# Example Morning Brief

A fully sanitized example of what a morning brief email looks like in practice. All names, places, and details are fictional. This is the **shape** of what you'll get — not real content.

---

**Subject:** 🌅 Morning Brief — Tuesday, June 9

---

## 🎯 Today's three

1. **The investor call at 10am.** You've been preparing this pitch for two weeks. Re-read the deck before 9:30 and refresh the financial slide — last week's update is in BACKLOG.md.
2. **Reply to the lawyer about the lease counter.** You've been sitting on this for 4 days. The longer you wait, the weaker your position. Draft is in `commitments.md` — review and send.
3. **Call your mother.** Last contact 9 days ago. She mentioned a doctor visit on Thursday. Today is when she wants the call, not later.

---

## 🧠 Insight

You've called your mother an average of every 11 days for the past three months. You used to do every 5. The drift isn't intentional — it's drift. The longer you let it slide, the harder the call gets, because there's more guilt baked in. Today, before noon. Don't make it a project.

---

**Wildcard:** The investor call you're nervous about isn't actually the hard thing today. The hard thing is the lawyer reply you've been avoiding for 4 days — because you don't actually know what you want from the lease counter, and not knowing makes you stall. The 10am pitch is performance. The lawyer reply is a decision. Make the decision first; it'll free up the rest of the day.
